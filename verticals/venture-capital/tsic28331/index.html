01

The investment instrument, at volume

Your edge is speed: 450+ cheques since 2017, written from day one. Every one still needs a clean SAFE, ASA or convertible, a term sheet and an executed instrument. That is a constant, repeating stream of paper, exactly the work that piles up on one desk when the fund is moving fast.

02

Side letters & MFN obligations

Fast, founder-friendly rounds mean side letters: information rights, pro-rata, most-favoured-nation. Each one you grant ripples across the fund, and MFN clauses have to be tracked and honoured across every LP and every deal, or they quietly compound into liability.

03

Fund formation & LP agreements

Raising and running the funds behind the cheques means LPAs, subscription documents and LP negotiations. The terms your LPs push at close have to stay consistent with how you actually deploy, and with what the next fund promises.

04

FCA regulation & financial promotions

Tiny Supercomputer Management (UK) is authorised and regulated by the FCA. That posture governs your financial promotions, your marketing to founders and LPs, and your AIFM and appointed-representative obligations. It is a live compliance surface, not a one-off filing.

05

UK + Germany, kept consistent

Two management entities, a UK Ltd and a German GmbH, mean two regulatory and tax regimes under one brand. Documents, disclosures and structures have to line up across both, so a deal in Berlin never contradicts a promise made in London.

06

KYC/AML, GDPR & SFDR disclosures

Onboarding founders and LPs at your pace means KYC/AML at volume across jurisdictions. Add the personal data flowing through a 900+ founder community under GDPR, and your published SFDR sustainability disclosures, and you have recurring compliance that has to stay current and defensible.
