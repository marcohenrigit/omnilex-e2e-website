A deep-tech vision company selling VIDAS into defense, heavy equipment and automotive, backed by Khosla Ventures and five DoD Direct-to-Phase-2 SBIR awards, carries a very specific legal footprint. From your public profile, here is where we would start.

01

Government and defense contracts

Your SBIR Phase II and Army CATALYST awards, and work through an integrator like RTX, come wrapped in FAR and DFARS clauses. The heart of it is data rights: keeping VIDAS as your commercial IP while the government takes only what it must. Get the rights and flow-downs wrong once and it follows every future contract.

02

Your VIDAS IP

VIDAS is the crown jewel. A clean patent strategy, watertight invention assignment, and trade-secret protection keep your algorithms yours, and give you freedom to operate against LiDAR incumbents and other vision patents as you scale.

03

Export control (ITAR and EAR)

Perception software adapted for warfighters is almost certainly export-controlled. You need the right classification, a technology control plan, license positions, and disciplined handling of foreign-national access and deemed exports, before an audit or a hire forces the question.

04

OEM and commercial agreements

Automotive and heavy-equipment deals turn on supply, integration and licensing terms, and on who carries the risk when a safety-critical perception system is in the loop. Warranty, liability caps and indemnities have to be drawn so a field failure never lands on Compound Eye.

05

Training-data and model rights

VIDAS is only as defensible as the data behind it. Labeling-vendor terms, data licensing, ownership of training sets and model outputs, and automotive-data privacy all need locking down so nobody else can claim a piece of what you built.

06

Team and partner protection

As you hire engineers and partner with OEMs and integrators, invention assignment, NDAs and trade-secret controls are what stop your edge from walking out the door or leaking across a partnership.
