We know your business

You broker the cover. This is the legal work that lands on your desk with it.

A commercial lines book carries a very specific legal footprint, one that grows with every account and every renewal. From how a HUB producer actually works, here is where we would start, and all of it recurs on every placement you touch.

01

Contract insurance-requirement review

Clients send you their MSAs, leases and vendor agreements asking whether their program meets the insurance and indemnity requirements. Reading each contract against their cover, and flagging the gaps, is constant, high-volume work.

02

Certificates, additional insured and waivers

Every account generates certificates of insurance, additional-insured endorsements and waivers of subrogation that have to match what the underlying contracts actually demand, not just what is convenient to issue.

03

Coverage and policy-wording analysis

Reading dense policy forms, endorsements and exclusions to confirm the cover does what the client was told it does, before a claim is the thing that proves otherwise.

04

Carrier, MGA and program agreements

The appointment, binding-authority and program agreements behind your book, kept consistent so what you can bind never drifts from what you have promised a client.

05

Surplus lines and regulatory compliance

Surplus-lines filings, state licensing and disclosure requirements that follow every non-admitted placement across the states you write in, each with its own rules and deadlines.

06

E&O exposure and documentation

The advice you give is the exposure you carry. Clean files, confirmed instructions and documented recommendations are what stand between a coverage dispute and an E&O claim.
