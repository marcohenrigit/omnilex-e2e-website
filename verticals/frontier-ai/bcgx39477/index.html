We know your business

You ship products, models and ventures. This is the legal layer under every build.

A build-and-design unit shipping AI systems, software and new companies for global enterprises carries a very specific legal footprint. From your public model and how BCG X operates, here is where we would start, and all of it recurs on every engagement.

01

IP ownership in every build

Who owns the code, the models and the designs you ship. Client SOWs and IP schedules have to allocate foreground and background IP cleanly, engagement after engagement, or ownership surfaces as a dispute exactly when a build succeeds.

02

The EU AI Act, built in

You deploy AI systems into regulated enterprises. Risk classification, transparency, human-oversight and technical-documentation duties attach to what you build, and they land on you as the provider, not only on the client.

03

Data and model-training rights

DPAs, data-use rights and training-data provenance for the data that powers your models, across client, third-party and open datasets, kept consistent so a model never ships on data you could not lawfully use.

04

Open-source licence compliance

Your builds pull in open-source components. Copyleft, attribution and patent-grant obligations have to be cleared before code ships to a client or into a venture, or the licence follows the product downstream.

05

Venture creation and spin-outs

When a build becomes a company: incorporation, IP assignment into NewCo, founder and equity terms, and investor and JV documents, structured so the venture stands clean on day one.

06

Client MSAs, SOWs and flow-downs

A constant stream of master agreements, statements of work and client-policy flow-downs gates every project kickoff, each one negotiated against a different client paper and a different risk posture.
