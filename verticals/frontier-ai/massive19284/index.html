We already looked at your legal surface

This isn't a cold template. Here's the exposure we'd expect on your desk.

A consented-bandwidth network selling real-time web access to AI teams carries a distinct legal footprint. From your public model, this is where we'd start — and what a pilot would touch first.

01

The SDK consent flow

Your whole "ethically sourced" claim lives or dies on the opt-in a user sees inside a partner app. Consent has to be specific, informed, revocable, and provable under GDPR and CCPA — and it has to survive AppEsteem, AMTSO and app-store review. If the disclosure is thin at the partner layer, the exposure lands on you.

02

Partner agreements & the consent chain

Every app publisher embedding the SDK is a link in your consent chain. Those contracts need consent warranties, disclosure obligations, reward terms, revocation handling and indemnities that actually flow risk back to the partner who onboarded the user — not to Massive.

03

Customer AUP & vetting

You sell access that a bad actor could misuse. An enforceable acceptable-use policy plus real customer vetting (KYC) is what separates "infrastructure provider" from "party to the misuse." This is the single highest-leverage document you can tighten.

04

Scraping risk, shifted correctly

Customers point your network at targets with their own terms of service. Your contracts should place responsibility for lawful use on the customer and give you a clean posture on target-ToS, CFAA-style and database-rights questions — without over-promising what you can police.

05

DPAs across partners & enterprise

You sit between consumer devices and enterprise buyers running security reviews. That means DPAs, sub-processor terms and SCCs on both sides, kept consistent with your SOC 2 and privacy posture so procurement doesn't stall your deals.

06

"Your data stays yours" — in writing

You promise customers their targets, volumes and strategy stay private. That promise is only as strong as the confidentiality and data-handling clauses behind it. We make the contract say exactly what your marketing says.

If any of these is already handled, we skip it. The pilot starts by confirming what's solid and pointing at what isn't — before we touch a document.
