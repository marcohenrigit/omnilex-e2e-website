We know your business

You built the ethical foundation for web data. This is the legal load underneath it.

A consented-bandwidth network selling real-time web access to AI teams, from Snowflake to Tavily and on the back of a Point72-led seed and a SOC 2 Type 1 posture, carries a very specific legal footprint. From your public model and your own SDK privacy policy, here is where we would start.

01

The SDK consent flow

Your entire “ethically sourced” promise lives on the opt-in a user sees inside a partner app. Your SDK policy commits to 100% opt-in, revocable consent, IP used only for routing, and nothing sold. That has to be specific, informed and provable across GDPR, CCPA, LGPD and India’s ITA-2000, and survive AppEsteem and antivirus-whitelisting review. If it is thin at the partner layer, the exposure lands on Massive.

02

Partner agreements & the consent chain

Every publisher embedding the Massive SDK is a link in your consent chain. Those contracts need consent warranties, disclosure duties, reward terms, opt-out and uninstall handling, and indemnities that push risk back to the partner who onboarded the user, not to Massive.

03

Customer AUP & vetting

You sell real-time access to any site on the internet. An enforceable acceptable-use policy plus real customer vetting (KYC) is what keeps “infrastructure provider” from becoming “party to the misuse”. It is the single highest-leverage document you can tighten.

04

Scraping liability, shifted correctly

Customers aim your network, and the antibot-bypassing Web Render API, at targets with their own terms of service. Your contracts should place lawful-use responsibility on the customer and hold a clean posture on target-ToS, CFAA-style and database-rights questions, without over-promising what you can police.

05

DPAs across partners & enterprise

You sit between consumer devices and enterprise buyers like Snowflake running security reviews. That means DPAs, sub-processor terms and SCCs on both sides, kept consistent with your SOC 2 Type 1 and privacy posture so procurement never stalls a deal.

06

“Your data stays yours”, in writing

You promise customers their targets, volumes and strategy stay private, and users that nothing is tracked or sold. Those promises are only as strong as the confidentiality and data-handling clauses behind them. We make the contracts say exactly what your marketing and SDK policy already claim.
